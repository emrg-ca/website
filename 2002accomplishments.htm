Accomplishments For 2002
       
    Participated in both local Amateur Hamfests
      • The Ottawa Amateur Radio Club (OARC) and the Ottawa Valley Mobile Radio Club (OVMRC)
      • provide a prime location free of charge at their fleamarket/hamfest.
    Participated in Emergency Preparedness Week Display
      • Sponsored by OCIPEP and City of Ottawa. Check out the pictures from the EMRG booth.
    Participated in Airport Exercise
      • Ottawa Airport staged a major exercise, integrated with City of Ottawa agencies, including Police, Fire, EMS, People Services, Red Cross, Hospitals, OC Transpo and EMRG. Here is a brief description of the exercise.


    Agreement for access to surplus radio equipment & radio sites

      • The City of Ottawa, Emergency Measures Unit and Fire Dept have been working with EMRG on a solution to make radio equipment, repeaters and antenna sites available to EMRG. This equipment is becoming available as the City of Ottawa, specifically Ottawa Fire, migrate to the EDACS trunked radio system.
    First year for membership list yearly cleanup
      • Accurate contact information is critical for EMRG, but this involves more than just adding names to a list and updating it when someone provides new information. There must be a way to remove names from the list when people move or are no longer interested. This process is now in place and there is someone managing it.
    3 general meetings, each with a training session
      • The main objective for 2002 was consistency, which meant a steady pace all year long, that was still going strong at the end of the year. Each of the general  meetings included a short administration section followed by a training session on communications.
    Participated in Hospital Disaster Planning Conference
      • The Hospital Disaster Planning Committee in Ottawa sponsors and annual conference. EMRG is a member of the planning committee and had an opportunity to set up a display booth to talk about Amateur radio as a resource to Hospitals and other agencies in an emergency.
    Established and distributed standard DC power connector
      • EMRG, in partnership with the City of Ottawa Emergency Measures Unit, selected a standard DC power connector to put on all equipment operated by EMRG. The City provided the initial stock of connectors, which have been installed on all radios in the EOC, Red Cross and are being installed on all spare equipment. Each EMRG member received a free connector to build an adapter to their equipment. Additional connectors are being sold to local Amateurs, which provided the funding to replenish the connector supply. Here is a picture of the standard connector.
    Establish VHF repeater (146.880-) at Fire Dispatch
      • EMRG has been re-assigned the VHF repeater frequency 146.880 MHz. This is now used in an operational repeater, located at the consolidated fire dispatch centre. The repeater antenna is on the tower and provides coverage through a large part of the City and even beyond the City to the East. This is part of the ongoing support from the City and Ottawa Fire.
    Establish basic 144, 220, 440 radios at Fire Dispatch
      • Ottawa Fire has included EMRG as part of their consolidated communications centre. EMRG has a room at the dispatch centre, for establishing Amateur radio communications. The first step is complete, which includes the capability to operate radios on three amateur bands. The development of the EMRG communications centre will continue as resources are made available, once Ottawa Fire moves to the trunked radio system.
