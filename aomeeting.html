Introduction

 

The meeting was called to order at 10:00 hrs.

 

The chair introduced President Smith, Vice-President Field Services Pierre Mainville, and Section Emergency Coordinator Bob Gammon.  The DECs and attending ECs and members at large then introduced themselves.

 

The Chair invited the SEC to take the podium and Bob extended a welcome

to those in attendance with special thanks and recognition to the RAC President Earle Smith VE6NM for his expression of goodwill and great spirit to deliver a position statement from the RAC Board related to the Ontario ARES Service.

 

Bob ended his introductory comments by asking for renewed dedication and a unified effort to use the assets and resources available to create the best ARES Program in Canada.

 

1.                  AC/ARES relationship and position statement.

 

Beginning with the formation of RAC from the former Canadian Division of the ARRL and CARF, President Smith delivered a forthright and very detailed historical account of HOW structural, administrative and leadership short shortcomings created the situation that resulted in the resignation of the Section Manager, her Assistant and the former SEC.  Making the point that delving into the WHY would not contribute in any fashion to a productive outcome, the President simply acknowledged RAC’s role and responsibility to address the situation.  He then read a policy statement outlining RAC’s support of the Field Service.  This statement will be released after confirmation at the next RAC Board of Directors meeting scheduled for 20 September.

 

VE3GIH asked about the contradictions between this statement, and a public statement the same week by the RAC First Vice President. Several members expressed appreciation for the position. VE3VY mentioned that a ‘one size fits all policy’ won’t work.  The differences between rural municipalities and the large urban areas are too great.  VE3ODR suggested the use of a mediator in resolving the differences with Toronto.  All feedback on the position statement should be sent to the RAC V.P. Field Services, Pierre Mainville.  VE6NM has had discussions with the RCMP about their proposed national emergency communications system.  Amateur Radio is expected to be a part of that system.  VE3JX warned against a dictatorial attitude as RAC begins to support ARES.

 

VE3RX thanked the RAC president for traveling from Grande Prairie AB to address the meeting and for reading the policy statement.

 

A copy of the statement is at Annex A.

 

2.                  Ontario ARES direction/leadership, and

3.                  Ontario ARES Director appointment.

 

Following President Smith’s departure, the chair then lead off discussion with a number of thoughts and considerations including the option of a non-profit organization. The latter would give ARES Ontario a recognized, legal structure that would better represent its interests to external organizations, and put ARES Ontario in a position where it would be possible to apply for public funding and/or grants.  Incorporation would also add protection against the misuse of the ARES Ontario name, and provide a basis on which to proceed if at some future date it was necessary to function completely independently.  Discussions centered on how this organization would be governed and function within the existing RAC structure.

 

Meeting adjourned 11:30 for lunch.  Meeting called to order at 12:40 hrs.

 

After a further hour’s discussion, it was moved by VE3VY, seconded by VE3ODR that:

 

"Whereas there appears to be a need to establish a registered non-profit association that speaks with one ARES Ontario voice to the Ontario provincial and municipal governments and other authorities, with the officers of said corporation to be made up of the SEC, & All DEC's.  Other directors may be invited at the discretion of the board.

 

“Further, that the corporation's terms of reference & constitution will be developed by a committee of those same officers.

 

“Further, that this corporation reaffirm its commitment to RAC."

 

The motion carried with two Nay votes.

 

The SEC then introduced the topic of appointing a Director for ARES Ontario.  After a review of the SEC’s Job Description (attached as Annex B), it was decided that the current SEC terms of reference already provided the needed authority and independence to fulfill the mission of ARES Ontario.

 

4.         Ontario ARES status with Service Agencies, i.e: EMO, OPP, Red Cross, Municipal CEMC’s.

 

VE3GIH informed the group about Scugog District’s provincial level contacts with the RCMP.  VE3RX has also made contact with them, to ensure that he is on their contact list in an emergency.  VE3ODR wanted improved provincial coordination of ARES activities.  VE3IPC stated that without an MOU, EMO may not really be our client.  He suggested that until we have an MOU with defined goals, our clients are really our independent Support Agencies and local communities.  However, a MOU would be valuable as the template for local MOUs.  VE3NY suggested that social service agencies be added to the list of service agencies.

 

There were discussions about the MOU with EMO.  The RAC VP Field Services told the group that discussions about a ‘1995 MOU’ were incorrect, and that there was only a letter, and not a Memorandum of Understanding.  The content of that March 27, 1995 letter are at Annex C.

 

5.         Toronto District and related issues.

 

VE3GIH stated that his only problem with ‘Toronto ARES’ is the possibility of competition.  If they are not putting AEC’s in his territory, and if OECO is not competing with ARES Ontario, then there isn’t a problem.  However, we should attempt to define those issues that we both can, and cannot, cooperate on.  Contact attempts that are made should include the entire Board of Directors of Toronto ARES.  VE3RX stated that there would be attempts made to contact Toronto ARES.  VA3QT noted that, as one of the 12 ARES Ontario districts, Toronto District had a place at the table and the equal voice of all Districts would bring balance to the urban/rural concerns that had been voiced.  Others present said that we should be trying to improve relations and cooperation with Toronto district. 

 

6.         Ontario ARES news media and public relations.

 

VE3VY suggested reprinting of “Amateur Radio Emergency Services,  Emergency Telecommunications”.  This blue pamphlet was previously printed by the provincial Office of the Solicitor General.  During the discussion it was brought out that government policy had changed with respect to printing documents for non-government organizations.  VA3PM noted that the original pamphlet was printed as a ‘thank-you’ for Amateur Radio support during the Ice Storm event.  It was concluded that the SEC should scan the document onto the http://www.aresontario.ca website so that members could reprint it themselves locally.

 

7.         Ontario ARES printing of information document and issuing a standard Provincial ARES ID card.

 

There was general agreement on the need for a standard ID card.  VA3PM demonstrated President Smith’s photo-ID badge provided by his home ARES group and informed the meeting that the topic of a RAC badge was to be put to the Board at its next meeting.  It was decided that:

 

a.      while a national standard was highly desirable a distinct ARES Ontario affiliation was also required; and,

 

b.      if in two months there was no decision on a national card then the SEC should implement an ARES Ontario card.

 

8.         Ontario ARES net operations HF/IRLP.

 

VE3RX said that he has been keeping careful track of the ARES Ontario nets.  The Wednesday evening net is less productive, in that it only has four or five districts represented by eight to ten stations checking in.  The Sunday evening net has better attendance, because seven to ten districts are represented by twenty-one to twenty-four stations checking in.  Propagation is not improving but reinstating the HF net still needs to be done this fall.  The objective of conducting the HF and IRLP nets simultaneously was discussed.

 

Details have to be worked out, and network controllers are needed.  VA3RX called for a volunteer to act as net manager.

 

VE3VY suggested, and it was agreed, that there should be topics for discussion during the net.

 

9.         New Communications Systems.

 

The need to exploit all communication modes had been covered in the previous discussion.  VA3QT noted that all modes should be kept in the “toolbox”.  In response to a question from VE3GIH he gave an overview of the Winlink System, Telpac operations, and the proposed system under discussion with Simcoe County officials. 

 

10.       Other Business.

 

VE3ODR is working on an arrangement with Twin Lakes Secondary School to allow students to do their 40 hours of community service by learning about amateur radio in an emergency communication environment.  He suggested that this would be a good way to get new public service members into amateur radio.

 

The annual SET is usually held the first weekend in October; however, it can be held anytime between September 1st and October 31st.  There will be no province-wide SET this year.  Individual ARES groups are welcome to perform their own SET.

 

11.       Amateur Radio and IRLP presentation by a pioneer of the subject, Dave, VA3BU.

 

Due to technical issues relating to the meeting room, VA3BU was unable to demonstrate his portable IRLP node.  He did discuss the application and utility of a portable IRLP node and answered a host of questions from the audience.  VA3BU also provided information on cross-linking Echolink with an IRLP node.

 

Adjournment

 

On behalf of all the participants VA3QT asked VA3PM to convey a very sincere thank-you to VE6NM for his extraordinary effort to personally deliver the RAC position statement.  As a token of appreciation, on behalf of the group an Emergency Measures Ontario lapel pin was handed over for mailing to President Smith along with his ARES ID badge (supplied by DEC Scugog).

 

The Chair expressed a sincere thank-you to (SEC) Bob, VA3RX, for having the vision and leadership to call the meeting.  He noted that the high level of optimism in the room in  combination with Bob’s initiative and President Smith’s support undoubtedly meant that ARES Ontario was well and truly on the track back to good health.

 

The meeting was adjourned at 1602 hours.

           Official Appointment Description

 

Section Emergency Coordinator (SEC)

 

            The SEC is the assistant to the SM for emergency preparedness. The SEC is appointed by the SM to take care of all matters pertaining to emergency communications and the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES) on a sectionwide basis. (Emphasis added.)  The SEC post is one of top importance in the section and the individual appointed to it should devote all possible energy and effort to this one challenging organizational program for Amateur Radio. There is only one SEC appointed in each section of the RAC Field Organization.

 

SEC qualifications and functions:

 

The encouragement of all groups of community amateurs to establish a local emergency organization.

 

Recommendation to the SM on all section emergency policy and planning, including the development of a section emergency communications plan.

 

Cooperation and coordination with the Section Traffic Manager so that emergency nets and traffic nets in the section present a united public service front, particularly in the proper routing of Welfare traffic in emergency situations. Cooperation and coordination should also be maintained with other section leadership officials as appropriate.

 

Recommendation of candidates for Emergency Coordinator and District Emergency Coordinator appointments (and cancellations) to the Section Manager and determine areas of jurisdiction of each amateur so appointed. At the SM's discretion, the SEC may be directly in charge of making (and canceling) such appointments. In the same way, the SEC can handle the Official Emergency Station program.

 

Promotion of ARES membership drives, meetings, activities, tests, procedures, etc., at the section level.

 

Collection and consolidation of Emergency Coordinator (or District Emergency Coordinator) monthly reports and submission of monthly progress summaries to the SM and RAC Headquarters. This includes the timely reporting of emergency and public safety communications rendered in the section for inclusion in TCA.

 

Maintenance of contact with other communication services and serve as liaison at the section level with all agencies served in the public interest, particularly in connection with federal, provincial and local government, civil preparedness, Red Cross, and so on.

 

            Your position description includes assisting your successor as much as possible to ensure a smooth transition in your position. This involves promptly updating and turning over the position records and supplies to your successor so that there is a little loss of continuity as possible. A business-like transition reflects well on your professionalism, responsibility and consideration as well as on RAC.

 

            Recruitment of new hams and RAC members is an integral part of the job of every appointee. Appointees should take advantage of every opportunity to recruit a new ham or member to foster growth of Field Organization programs, and our abilities to serve the public.

 

Requirement: The Section Emergency Coordinator is required to be a Full RAC member and hold an Amateur Operator’s Certificate (or equivalent, as stipulated by the Radiocommunication Regulations) and should always operate radio equipment only within the limits and privileges of the certificate and qualification held.
