Hello

Here are a few EMRG updates.

  1. EMRG will not participate in the SET this year. We have some things we need to test this fall, but they are not all ready, so we will wait until they are ready.
  2. VHF repeater VA3OFS is operational in Barrhaven. It is still getting some work, so it is a bit deaf, but this will be corrected over the next couple weeks.
  3. On October 13, team leader Peter Gamble gave an hour long presentation about EMRG to the City of Ottawa Emergency Planning Working Group. This group has representatives from all the City departments. (More later)
  4. The next EMRG general meeting is on Saturday Jan 28 at the Ottawa Fire Training Centre on Industrial Ave. The EMRG management team is planning this meeting, so I get to take it easy. More information in the newsletter next month.
  5. The VE3TEL UHF repeater that was on Bank St, has been shut down by the Telephone Pioneers. They donated the repeater to EMRG and it has now been moved to Fire Dispatch. The equipment was installed in a relay rack a few weeks ago and the next step is to wire up the controller and get it back on the air as VE3OCE UHF.
  6. The Red Cross radio project is progressing. I am nearly finished building the cabinet. The cabinet had to be only 12" wide to fit in the space available, and must hold 5 radios, a TNC, a power supply and a battery. Each piece of equipment is on a sliding shelf, so it can be removed for repair or replacement easily. I designed and built the cabinet, but I will be getting others to do the wiring and help with the installation. (More later)
  7. We have had one evening work session at Randall, and we have more work planned. The projects have been delayed this fall due to the work on the items above. Most of the items above had to be done on some else's schedule, not ours.
  8. Tom Zink and Brad Smith have been doing some packet radio testing. Also the Prescott Russell ARES group has been testing. If you have a TNC, dig it out and give it a try on 145.030. The digipeater is VE3OCE and there are stations to connect to; VE3OCE-1, VE3BQP-7 and a Winlink Telpac node VE3BQP-1.

====================================================================

That is all for now!

December 18, 2004

Peter Gample signed a new 3 year agreement with the City of Ottawa this week, on behalf of EMRG. The agreement is the same as before, regarding expectations and limitations of what EMRG provides. There is a new portion of the agreement which will provide a one time cash transfer to EMRG this year of $5000, with $1000 each year for the remaining 2 years of the agreement.

This is good news for EMRG, allowing us to move forward with the projects we have been talking about for the last year or two. The agreement is also signed by Steve Kanellakos, Deputy City Manager for Community and Protective services. This agreement is a result of the strong relationship we have with the City and their belief in, and support of, the work we are doing.

The EMRG management team is in the process of setting up a bank account for the money and we have created a set of documents which define the rules on how money is managed and spent. You can check out the documents on the EMRG web site at

Official Documents

Look for Administration documents; 305 and 312, as well as forms; 510 and 511.

December 7, 2004

The EMRG standard radio Interface document (EMRG-210) has been updated significantly based on local and outside feedback. The latest copy is posted on the EMRG web site at: Standard Radio Interface or you can go to the EMRG home page at http://www.emrg.ca and there is a link to the standardization section.

The website now has a section dedicated to standardization, which includes some preliminary ideas on building a standard user interface.

Please take a look and let me know what you think, what can be improved or just let me know you read the document.
