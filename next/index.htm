The Emergency Measures Radio Group (EMRG) is made up of Federally licensed Amateur radio operators, located in the City of Ottawa, who have made a commitment to volunteer their time & equipment in the event of an emergency. EMRG is a local volunteer radio organization, also known as Ottawa ARES . Two names, one group. one purpose.

The main role for EMRG is to provide radio communications in an emergency to organizations involved in humanitarian response, who do not have a radio system, lack capacity on their system or who normally rely on phones and cell phones.

The three main humanitarian response groups in Ottawa are the City of Ottawa Community Services Department, Ottawa Red Cross, and the Salvation Army. EMRG radio communications would be used to link shelters, mobile care teams, community patrols, the Emergency Operations Centre and other key sites as part of the humanitarian response.

EMRG provides a single point of contact to access the volunteer radio operators in Ottawa, as well as interfacing with neighbouring radio groups. EMRG has direct relationships with the City of Ottawa Emergency Management Unit, the Ottawa Red Cross, and the Hospitals in Ottawa.
