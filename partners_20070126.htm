EMRG Partners

Ottawa Red Cross

The Ottawa branch of the Canadian Red Cross and the Emergency Measures Radio Group have a signed Memorandum Of Understanding (MOU). Through this agreement, the Red Cross will have access to the communications capabilities of EMRG. EMRG operates and maintains the radio equipment located at the Red Cross offices on Catherine Street.

Radio Amateurs of Canada (RAC) and the National Red Cross have a high level MOU in which RAC will provide communications capabilities to the Red Cross, through the local ARES group.

Ottawa Airport

The Ottawa International Airport is controlled by the MacDonald-Cartier International Airport Authority. In a major Airport emergency or disaster, the Emergency Measures Radio Group (EMRG) would typically provide communications for organizations supporting the relief effort in the community, not radio operators at the airport. Volunteer radio operators would deploy to support the City of Ottawa Community Services and the Ottawa Red Cross with shelters, backup communications for the Hospitals if required, or as additional support for logistics such as food services for agencies at the Airport.

The Ottawa Airport Fire Chief has been an important promoter of EMRG within the Emergency Response community. In May 2002, EMRG participated in the Airport Exercise Integration which involved all aspects of Emergency Response in Ottawa, including People Services, Red Cross and the Hospitals.

Ottawa Hospitals

The Emergency Measures Radio Group (EMRG) is a member of the Hospital Disaster Planning Committee of Ottawa. The committee meets regularly to discuss issues related to hospital disaster planning. Members of the committee include hospital emergency planners, as well as representatives from all other key agencies such as Police, Fire, EMS, EMU, Red Cross, etc.

EMRG's role in an emergency is to augment or provide backup communications for the intra-hospital radio system, which is used to connect the hospital disaster planning teams together and to communicate with the Emergency Measures Unit and EMS.

The members of the Hospital Disaster Planning Committee of Ottawa are:

  • Ottawa Hospital (General Campus, Civic Campus, and Riverside Campus)
  • Queensway Carleton Hospital (QCH)
  • Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario (CHEO)
  • Montfort Hospital
  • Ottawa Heart Institute
  • Royal Ottawa Hospital
  • Rehab Institute
  • Base Hospital Program
  • Ottawa Community Access Centre
  • Ottawa Red Cross
  • Public Health and Long Term Care
  • Central Ambulance Communications Centre (CACC)
  • City of Ottawa Emergency Measures Unit (EMU)
  • MacDonald-Cartier International Airport Authority
  • Emergency Medical Services (EMS)
  • Ottawa Fire Dept (OFD)
  • Ottawa Police Service (OPS)

City of Ottawa

The emergency communications services of EMRG are available to the City of Ottawa and EMRG is included in the City's Official Emergency Plan (May 2002) , on page 16. EMRG reports to the Emergency Measures Unit (EMU), but would typically be used to provide communications for Community Services, the City department responsible to operate and manage shelters in an emergency.

In the event that more than one group or agency has requested the services of EMRG, the response priority is always the City of Ottawa Emergency Measures Unit.

In a situation where more than one group or agency is requesting EMRG assistance, it is probably a common emergency and the groups or agencies are working with the City of Ottawa. EMRG resources would be prioritized through the Emergency Measures Unit, in consultation with other groups, to provide the most effective use possible.

EMRG has an excellent relationship with the City of Ottawa, through various City of Ottawa departments. EMU and Community Services are the primary groups EMRG works with. The Ottawa Fire Service (OFS) is an important partner, providing infrastructure for EMRG operations within the City. OFS provides space for the EMRG communications room and equipment storage, as well as access to antennas within the City for EMRG radio repeaters.

Providing backup communications for Public Safety radio systems is NOT a mandate for EMRG. If the City of Ottawa radio system used by Police and Fire failed, or if the Provincial radio system used by Paramedics failed, EMRG would attempt to assist as much as possible. It must be understood that there are not sufficient radios, or radio infrastructure (repeaters) for EMRG to support more than a few key sites, which is significantly less than the several thousand radios and over 50 sites they normally use. This is why the radio systems used by these agencies cost so much, because they have built in fail safe systems to ensure that the system can operate even in a reduced mode if something should happen to the system.

EMRG can provide communications for Police, Fire or Paramedics, to support Command & Control, and Interoperability, by linking key sites that are without phone or radio communications, or offload existing systems of non critical communications. This would involve limited sites with specific purposes for the communications.

The current City of Ottawa was formed on January 1, 2001, by the merger of the Regional Municipality of Ottawa-Carleton and 11 local Municipalities.
