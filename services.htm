The Emergency Measures Radio Group (EMRG) is made up of Federally licensed Amateur radio operators, located in the City of Ottawa, who have made a commitment to volunteer their time and equipment in the event of an emergency. EMRG is a local volunteer radio organization, also known as Ottawa ARES . Two names, one group. one purpose.

The main role for EMRG is to provide radio communications in an emergency to organizations involved in humanitarian response, who do not have a radio system, lack capacity on their system or who normally rely on phones and cell phones.

The three main humanitarian response groups in Ottawa are:

  • the City of Ottawa Community Services Department
  • the Ottawa Red Cross
  • the Salvation Army

As part of the humanitarian response, EMRG radio communications would be used to link:

  • shelters
  • mobile care teams
  • community patrols
  • the Emergency Operations Centre
  • other key sites

EMRG provides a single point of contact to access the trained volunteer radio operators in Ottawa, as well as interfacing with neighbouring radio groups.

EMRG has direct relationships with:

  • the City of Ottawa Emergency Management Unit
  • the Ottawa Red Cross
  • the Hospitals in Ottawa
