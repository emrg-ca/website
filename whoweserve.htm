City of Ottawa

The emergency communications services of EMRG are available to the City of Ottawa and EMRG is included in the Cities' official emergency plan. EMRG reports to the Emergency Measures Unit (EMU), but would typically be used to provide communications for other agencies such as People Services and the Red Cross.

The current City of Ottawa was formed on January 1, 2001, by the merger of the Regional Municipality of Ottawa-Carleton and 11 local Municipalities.

Red Cross

The Ottawa branch of the Canadian Red Cross and the Emergency Measures Radio Group have a signed Memorandum Of Understanding (MOU). Through this agreement, the Red Cross will have access to the communications capabilities of EMRG.

Radio Amateurs of Canada and the National Red Cross have a high level MOU in which RAC will provide communications capabilities to the Red Cross, through the local ARES group.

Ottawa Hospitals

The Emergency Measures Radio Group (EMRG) is a member of the Hospital Disaster Planning Committee of Ottawa. The committee meets regularly to discuss issues related to hospital disaster planning. Members of the committee include hospital emergency planners, as well as representatives from all other key agencies such as Police, Fire, EMS, EMU, Red Cross, etc.

EMRG's role in an emergency is to augment or provide backup communications for the intra-hospital radio system, which is used to connect the hospital disaster planning teams together and to communicate with the Emergency Measures Unit and EMS.

Ottawa International Airport

The Ottawa International Airport is controlled by the MacDonald-Cartier International Airport Authority. In a major Airport emergency or disaster, the Emergency Measures Radio Group (EMRG) would typically provide communications for organizations supporting the relief effort in the community, not radio operators at the airport. Volunteer radio operators would deploy to support the City of Ottawa People Services and the Ottawa Red Cross with shelters, backup for the Hospitals if required or as additional support for logistics such as food services for agencies at the Airport.
